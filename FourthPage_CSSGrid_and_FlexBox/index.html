    1. Neo-futuristic Architecture


    • Riverside Museum, Glasgow, 2011.
    • Walt Disney Concert Hall, US, 1999.
    • Heydar Aliyev Centre, Baku, Azerbaijan, 2013.

    Unique features of Neo-Futurism
    A style seen as a more idealistic approach to the future. Defined by physics-defying angles, curvature, and spirals that shoot up to the sky and the use of new and different material like glass, aluminum and steel all for a more sleek, modern, and technological appeal that aims - almost literally - for the stars.

    Pioneers of Neo-Futuristic Architecture
    Pioneered by many talented individuals, some well-known names include Zaha Hadid, with her designs such as the London Aquatics Centre and the Riverside Museum, Santiago Calatrava, known for his out-of-the-box bridge designs including the Gare do Oriente and Samuel Beckett Bridge, Suraksha Acharya, who has won two awards for designing an extraordinarily futuristic skyscraper - the Aero Hive.

    A Push to New Heights
    Neo-futuristic designs pushes the limits of the idea of modern architecture to great heights. With the ever-growing age of technology and innovation, the question about "what is possible?" keeps on being pushed to new heights.

    2. Modern Architecture


    • Su Casa Design, British Columbia.
    • A modern contemporary architectually designed house, Spain.
    • A modern home, somewhere.

    Unique features of Modern Architecture
    With an aim to prioritise simplicity of form, clean structure, lack of ornamentation, and function over form, this style's approach is most suitable for everyday living and is why it has seen the most attention in recent times.

    Pioneers of Modernistic Architecture
    It follows that the most iconic examples of modern architecture include Frank Lloyd Wright's Fallingwater house in the United States, Le Corbusier's Villa Savoye in France, and Ludwig Mies Van Der Rohe's Neue Nationalgalerie in Berlin.

    A Stay at Minimalism
    This architectural style became an umbrella term that encompasses several different styles that became prominent during the first half of the 20th century. A minimalist style at heart; it became practiced commonly by many architects until after World War 2.

    3. Post-Modern Architecture


    • Kindergarten Wolfartsweier, Karlsruhe, Germany, 2002.
    • Dancing House, Prague, Czech Republic
    • M2 Building, Tokyo, Japan, 1991.

    Unique features of Post-Modern Architecture
    Refusing to be boxed as one type of category, the Post-Modernistic approach consists of a mix of architectural styles of a somewhat hybrid and whimsical design often with animal or life-like features that manifests a very vivid personality.

    Pioneers of Post-Modernistic Architecture
    The Vanna Venturi House in Pennsylvania, USA designed by Robvert Venturi is one of the first prominent architecture structures of the post-modern architecture movement. Two other famous examples are the Guggenheim Museum in Bilbao, Spain and the Dancing House in Prague.

    A Revival of the Arts
    Opposing the plain austerous and rigid design of the modern approach, the Post-Modern architects launched this movement in the 1960s. With the growing power of post-modernism in about everything and the revsioning of the way of thinking, this stylistic effort can be seen as a continuation of the Renaissance.
